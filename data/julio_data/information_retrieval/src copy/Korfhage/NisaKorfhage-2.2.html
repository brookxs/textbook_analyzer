2.2 Data Structures in the Large

Structure of a document affects the storage format of the documents and the modes of access. There are two kinds of format:

  1. Fully formatted documents
    • Includes data found in relational, network, or hierarchical databases.
    • Each document consists of predefines number of fields that has a predefined size and position in the document.
    • Excellent for exact retrieval of exact data or data falling within a specific range of values.
    • Less appropriate for storage of imprecise data and for response to imprecise queries.
    Fully unformatted data
    • Includes documents for which the only structure is the order imposed by the process of recording them.
    • Retrieval depends on complete scans of the document set.

Many documents are an intermediate form or mixed form, largely unformatted but including some formatted portions. Therefore, two retrieval processes might be the most efficient - that is by rough retrieval based on formatted portion of the document and then refining the unformatted to locate the desired items.
