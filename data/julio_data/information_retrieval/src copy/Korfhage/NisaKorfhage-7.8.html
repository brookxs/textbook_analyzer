7.8 Summary

GUIDO and VIBE (in both vector and Boolean forms) are designed to allow the use approach the system with several different views in mind:

  • by presenting to the user all documents that are in any way related to these point of view.
  • by providing a structured display of documents through which the user can browse freely.

These systems give the user the dynamic control over the definitions of the reference points. BIRD provides similar, but more limited approach to meeting information needs.

These three systems emphasize different aspects of information processing. GUIDO focuses on the problem of retrieving document sets. VIBE uses the similarity ratios and this reduces its effectiveness as a retrieval tool. BIRD focuses on constructing bibliographic lists of documents with interface most directly tied to the ultimate information need.
