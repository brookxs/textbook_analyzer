2.6 Data Compression

Data compression is needed to minimize the time required to search a database that is large in size. Document surrogates may be regard as a crude form of data compression, since they represent relatively large documents in small number of bytes.

One compression technique used is stemming, or the removal of grammatical suffixes, converting a set of related words to a common root form.

Two decisions may impact the effect of any data compression:

  1. Level of compression - compression can be done either at the character or word level.
  2. Data model used includes:
    • A static model - constructed by examining a sample of text and constructing statistical table representing this sample.
    • An adaptive model - begins with a priori statistical distribution for the text symbols but modifies the distribution as each character or word is encoded.
    • Semi-static model - a compromise between static model and the adaptive model.

There are three types of codes used for text compression:

  1. Huffman code - is based on the frequency distribution of the symbols to be encoded, whether characters or words.
  2. Ziv-Lempel codes - is based on identifying each text segment the first time and simply pointing back to the first occurrence rather than repeating the segment.
  3. Arithmetic codes - is based on the text stream represented by a number whose digits reflect the statistical frequency distribution of the symbols.
