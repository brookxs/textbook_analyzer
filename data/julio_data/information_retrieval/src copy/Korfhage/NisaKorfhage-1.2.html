1.2 The Information System

An information system is composed of two major portions, the ectosystem and the endosystem. Ectosystem consists of factors that are not under the control of the system designer. Ectosystem has three main components:

  1. The user - the person stores or retrieves information from the information system.
  2. The funder - the person or organization who underwrites the cost of operating the information system.
  3. The server - the information professional who operates the system and provides service to the users.

Endosystem consists of factors that a designer can specify and control that has four components:

  1. Media - includes various forms of hard copy, such as printed text and machine-readable media, such as magnetic tapes and disks, and CD-ROMs.
  2. Devices - ranges from file drawers to digital computers, scanners and laser-based processing equipment.
  3. Algorithms - the most efficient one is chosen for processing stored information and queries for an effective and efficient information system.
  4. Data structures - chosen based on algorithms and applications of the system.
