2.3 Document Surrogates

Document surrogate is a limited representation of full documents. Information retrieval focuses how to generate and evaluate document surrogates in response to information need.

Document surrogate is regard as document identifier as provide means of linking to the full document Identifier provides little information about the full documents that it is typically not useful response to information needs. Typically other data such as titles, dates and names are generally considered to be useful to the user and is included in the document surrogate.

Other information is included in the document surrogates to increase its usefulness in effective information retrieval:

  1. Keyword - one of a set of individual words chosen by the author or editor, or automatically, to represent the content of a document.
  2. Key phrase - phrases chosen to represent the content of the document.
  3. Abstract - a brief one- or two- paragraph description of the contents of a paper. A well-written abstract enable reader to determine whether the entire paper should be retrieved.
  4. Extracts - similar to an abstract that it is written by someone other than the author.
