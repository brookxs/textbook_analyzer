10.1 Natural Language Processing

Natural language processing involves restricting the term to processing that involves syntactic, pragmatic, or dialogue processing of documents.

Syntactic analysis is the analysis of a text to determine its syntactic structure. The syntactic structure of a document is the way in which it is written, following the grammatical or structural rules of the language. Thus the syntax of a sentence is focused on its structure rather than its meaning.

Syntactic analysis suffers from a number of problems:

  • Syntactic ambiguity - the existence of sentences that can be assigned more than one syntactic structure.
  • Deep structure - the structure of a sentence related to its meaning, independent of the specific syntax used.
  • There is no guarantee in which a sentence that is syntactically correct is semantically correct.
  • Syntactic approach assumes that the language to be handled is syntactically correct.

Semantic analysis involves analyzing a text to determine its meaning. In retrieval system, there is a need to match the meaning of a document to the meaning of a query. Semantic structure of a document is the way sentences are structure based on the meaning. One semantic analysis technique is the latent semantic indexing that uses multidimensional scaling methods to identify major concepts in a document, in contrast to simply the vocabulary words used.
