10.2 Citation Processing

Citation processing is a way to locate a set of related documents such as the bibliography or list of reference that a document cites. Cited documents can be used to enhance the description of a primary document, or comparing the vocabularies of citing. It can also be used as a measure of similarity.

Other researchers have examined co-citation as a measure of document similarity. This involves the concept that the similarity of two documents can be measured by the number of papers that cites both the documents. Bibliographic coupling occurs when two documents cite the same document.
