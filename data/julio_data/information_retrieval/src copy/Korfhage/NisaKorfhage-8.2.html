8.2 Precision and Recall, and Related Measures

Precision is defined as the proportion of retrieved documents. Recall is defines as the proportion of relevant documents that are retrieved. Precision and recall are both bounded above by 1 and below by 0. In general precision and recall has an inverse relationship. Precision falls and recalls rises as the number of documents retrieved in response to a query is increased. This relationship can be demonstrated in a smoothed precision-recall graph. Retrieval of a relevant document increases both precision and recall, while retrieval of a nonrelevant document decreases precision but does not affect recall.

Fallout is the proportion of nonrelevant documents that are not retrieved. Generality is the proportion of relevant documents within the entire collection.

There are there major problem of precision recall measures:

  • While precision can be determined exactly, recall cannot.
  • It is not clear that recall and precision are significant to the user.
  • Recall and precision are related to each other, and each alone provides an incomplete picture of the retrieval systems.
