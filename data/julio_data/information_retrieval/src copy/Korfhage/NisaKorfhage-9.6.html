9.6 The TREC Experiments

The TREC experiments provide a new venue for research on improving retrieval effectiveness. Much has been learned from the first series of experiments (TREC-1) about handling large, full-text databases in a retrieval situation and about how to evaluate the results. TREC-1 experiment used retrieval techniques ranging from simple Boolean to sophisticated natural processing.

In TREC-2 experiments, additional databases were incorporated and relevance feedback was made a factor in the experiments. The total number of relevant documents returned increased.

TREC-3 introduced new topics with shorter descriptions, allowing for more innovative topic expansion ideas. TREC-4 continued experiments with groups working on interactive searching and multilingual search tasks.
