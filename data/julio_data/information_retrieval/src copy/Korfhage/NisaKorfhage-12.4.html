12.4 Distributed Document Systems

Cost, efficiency, and shear number of documents being published are driving information systems to the use of distributed document set and distributed processing.

However, distributed document systems raise three problems:

  • Different databases have different formats and processing requirements. Therefore, the system that accesses these must address the disparities.
  • Data redundancy is likely to happen where different databases may have the same copies of documents.
  • Matching document evaluations from the distributed systems is difficult since different systems may rate a given document according to different evaluation functions.
