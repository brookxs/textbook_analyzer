8.3 User-Oriented Measures

There are three user-oriented measure:

  1. Coverage ratio - the proportion of the relevant documents known to the user that are actually retrieved.
  2. Novelty ratio - the proportion of the relevant retrieved documents that were previously unknown to the user.
  3. Relative recall - the ratio of the relevant retrieved documents examined by the user to the number of documents the user would like to examine.

High novelty ratio suggests that the system is effective in locating documents previously unknown to the user. Relative recall addresses more directly the question of how many documents the user wants.

Another user-related measure is called the recall effort, that is, the ratio of the number of relevant documents desired to the number of documents examined by the user to find the number of relevant documents desired.
