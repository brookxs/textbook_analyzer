4.7 Fuzzy Matching

Fuzzy matching appears similar to probability matching but replaces the need to estimate probabilities by a need to estimate a sense of belief about a document being relevant. To compute the fuzzy set, simple functions of membership grades for each document is used.

Different approaches can be taken to the concept of fuzziness. In considering semantically related terms, one might consider how well a related term matches a given term. Another approach is to look for modifiers or descriptors that give some indication of the value of the information in a document. Fuzzy matching can be combined with other matching process, such as the weighted Boolean approach.
