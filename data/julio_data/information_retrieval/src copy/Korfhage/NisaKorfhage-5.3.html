5.3 Term Extraction and Analysis

Frequency variation is one basis foe selection of words and phrases as automatic indexing terms. Numerous studies show that if the words in a text are ranked in order or decreasing frequency, they follow a relationship known as Zipf's law:

Rank X frequency = constant

The law implies that the frequency of words in a text falls off very rapidly for the most frequently occurring words, then more and more gradually as words occur more rarely.

Zipf's law makes 2 observations:

  1. The most common words have little specific relationship to the content (topic) of documents within a given collection.
  2. There will likely be a large number of words that occur only once, twice, three times within a document collections.
