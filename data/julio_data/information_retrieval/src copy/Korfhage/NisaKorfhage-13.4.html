13.4 Copyright Issues

Intellectual property rights, or copyright is the right of the creator of a document to recognition and benefit from his or her work. Copyrights usually prohibit any quotations from a document without obtaining explicit permission. Against this warning stands the doctrine of use that defines:

  1. That individuals can make private use of information that they wish to make limited public, non profit use of information as long as due credit is given.
  2. The warning notwithstanding, information such as the title, author, and publication data can be freely used in book catalogs, reviews, and bibliographic citations.
