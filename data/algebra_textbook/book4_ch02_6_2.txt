The Commutative Property of Multiplication Commutative Property of Multiplication The product of two whole numbers is the same regardless of the order of the factors. Sample Set A Example 2.35.  Multiply the two whole numbers. 6 ⋅ 7 = 42 7 ⋅ 6 = 42 The numbers 6 and 7 can be multiplied in any order. Regardless of the order they are multiplied, the product is 42.
