investigating influential factors influencing users to share news in social-media : a diffusion-of-innovations perspective this study aims to investigate the factors influencing news-sharing in social-media . drawing from the diffusion-of-innovations theory (doi) , the influential factors identified are opinion leadership , homophily , tie-strength , and news attributes . by incorporating social-network-analysis with multiple-regression-analysis , our results indicate that opinion leadership was the strongest factor predicting users ' news-sharing , followed by news attribute and tie-strength . unexpectedly , we also found that homophily hampered news-sharing in social-media . implications are discussed
