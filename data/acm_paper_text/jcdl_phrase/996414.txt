mediating team-work for digital-heritage archiving building digital-heritage requires substantial resources in materials , expertise , tools , and cost . projects supported by governments and academics can only cover a small part of the world 's heritage in both time and space dimensions . the preservation coverage-problem is most serious in domains where sources of intellectual and cultural-heritage may diminish or disappear over time . a central notion that helps resolve these issues is to facilitate global reach of digital-technology to sources of valuable heritage . we propose an approach to exploit non-institutional resources for wider participation and coverage in digital-heritage endeavor . the approach attempts to replicate institutional digital-heritage work by teaming up non-institutional resources and providing standard practice .
