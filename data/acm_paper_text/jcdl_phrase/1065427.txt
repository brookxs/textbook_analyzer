language preference in a bi-language digital-library this paper examines user choice of interface language in a bi-language digital-library (english and maori , the language of the indigenous-people of new-zealand) . the majority of collection documents are in maori , and the interface is available in both maori and english . log-analysis shows three categories of preference for interface language : primarily english , primarily maori , and bilingual (switching back and forth between the two) .
