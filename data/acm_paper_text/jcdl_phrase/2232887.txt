longitudinal-analysis of historical texts ' readability digital-libraries often contain historical-documents of varying age . the degree to which users can understand their content depends much on their reading-difficulty . in this poster paper we report the results of our studies on the readability of historical-documents from the viewpoint of present users . we investigate the correlation between the outcomes of different readability measurements and publication dates of prose texts on the basis of two datasets , the victorian women 's writers project and the corpus of late modern-english texts .
