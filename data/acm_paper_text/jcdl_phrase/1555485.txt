analyzing opac-use with screen views and eye-tracking eye-tracking was used to analyze which elements of which screens were viewed by users searching an online public access catalog (opac) . eye-tracking-data was obtained for 32 participants performing a known-item search-task . the results show that more than 30 \ % of participants did not make effective use of screens offering additional details , and that participants who did , and found the correct answer , gazed at specific screen elements more frequently than participants who gave incorrect answers .
