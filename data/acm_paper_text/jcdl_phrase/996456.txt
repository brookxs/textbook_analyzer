dlist : opening lis research-and-practice in this paper we describe dlist , a-digital library for library-and-information-science research-and-practice and for information-technology as it relates to lis it is built upon the open-access eprints model , but that extends materials in the collection beyond the formal , scholarly literature to include other types of content created by researchers and practitioners . dlist is intended to promote resource-sharing in lis and it and to attempt to bridge the gap between research-and-practice . the notion of open-access is briefly discussed as a central tenet for the development of the intellectual commons as an interactive-space for learning .
