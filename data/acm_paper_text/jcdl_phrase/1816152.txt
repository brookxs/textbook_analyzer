interactively browsing movies in terms of action , foreshadowing and resolution we describe a novel video-player that uses temporal semantic compression (tsc) to present a compressed summary of a movie . compression is based on tempo which is derived from film rhythms . the technique identifies periods of action , drama , foreshadowing and resolution , which can be mixed in different amounts to vary the kind of summary presented . the compression-algorithm is embedded in a video-player , so that the summary can be interactively recomputed during playback .
