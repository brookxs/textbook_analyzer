a study of how online-learning resource are used this paper defines a model of teacher practice (`` teaching as design '') , and describes a professional-development curriculum in which k-12-teachers design-learning activities using resources and tools from education-digital-libraries . it then presents preliminary findings from an application of this model in which teachers ' artifacts are analyzed to learn how online-learning-resources are used in-situ . initial results suggest that learning-resources of a smaller granularity are more likely to be adapted or improvised upon in teacher-designed learning-activities , which further supports teachers ' becoming contributors of online-resources and active participants in an education cyberinfrastructure .
