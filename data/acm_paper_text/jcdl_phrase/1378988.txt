interface effects on digital-library credibility-judgments in digital-library search-engines , `` no-results-found '' is a misleading phrase because it masquerades as a definitive answer ; in reality , the collection being searched may in fact contain content that matches a user 's query . this research examines the effect of null-result-sets on search-behavior and on the perception of contents in digital-libraries . in particular , this research supports the hypothesis that interface and design-flaws have an effect on the perceived authority and credibility (here defined in terms of being authentic , factual , trustworthy , scholarly , and accurate) of the information being communicated by the interface in question . in short , interface-design and the `` form '' of information (or , alternatively , the messenger) can negatively impact the perception of the quality of the `` content '' of information (the message) .
