finding unusual review patterns using unexpected rules in recent years , opinion-mining attracted a great deal of research attention . however , limited work has been done on detecting opinion-spam (or fake-reviews) . the problem is analogous to spam in web-search [1 , 9 11] . however , review-spam is harder to detect because it is very hard , if not impossible , to recognize fake-reviews by manually reading them [2] . this paper deals with a restricted problem , i.e. , identifying unusual review patterns which can represent suspicious behaviors of reviewers . we formulate the problem as finding unexpected rules . the technique is domain-independent . using the technique , we analyzed an amazon.com review dataset and found many unexpected rules and rule groups which indicate spam activities .
