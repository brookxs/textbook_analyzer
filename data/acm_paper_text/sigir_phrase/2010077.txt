cognitive coordinating behaviors in multitasking web-search this paper investigates how users cognitively coordinate multitasking web-search across different information-search problems . the analysis suggests that (1) multitasking is a prevalent web-search-behavior including both sequential multitasking (31 \ %) and parallel multitasking (69 \ %) ; (2) multitasking is performed through a task-switching process ; and (3) such a process is supported and underpinned by cognitive-coordination mechanisms and strategy coordination .
