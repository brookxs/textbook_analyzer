cumulative citation-recommendation : classification vs. ranking cumulative citation-recommendation refers to the task of filtering a time-ordered corpus for documents that are highly relevant to a predefined set of entities . this task has been introduced at the trec knowledge-base acceleration track in 2012 , where two main families of approaches emerged : classification and ranking . in this paper we perform an experimental-comparison of these two strategies using supervised-learning with a rich feature-set . our main finding is that ranking outperforms classification on all evaluation settings and metrics . our analysis also reveals that a ranking-based approach has more potential for future improvements .
