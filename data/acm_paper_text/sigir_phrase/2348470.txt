explaining neighborhood-based recommendations recommender-systems (rs) attempt to discover users ' preferences , and to learn about them in order to anticipate their needs . the main task normally associated with a rs is to offer suggestions for items . however , for most users , rss are black boxes , computerized oracles that give advice , but can not be questioned . in order to improve the quality of predictions and the satisfaction of the users , explanations facilities are needed . we present a novel methodology to explain recommendations : showing predictions over a set of observed items . our proposal has been validated by means of user-studies and lab experiments using movielens dataset .
