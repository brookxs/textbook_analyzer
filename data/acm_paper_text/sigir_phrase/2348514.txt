text selections as implicit-relevance-feedback users ' search activity has been used as implicit-feedback to model-search interests and improve the performance of search systems . in search-engines , this behavior usually takes the form of queries and result clicks . however , richer data on how people engage with search-results can now be captured at scale , creating new opportu-nities to enhance search . in this poster we focus on one type of newly-observable behavior : text-selection events on search-result captions . we show that we can use text selections as implicit-feedback to significantly improve search result relevance .
