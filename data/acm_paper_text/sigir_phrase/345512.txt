relevance and contributing information-types of searched documents in task-performance end-users base the relevance-judgements of the searched documents on the expected contribution to their task of the information contained in the documents . there is a shortage of studies analyzing the relationships between the experienced contribution , relevance-assessments and type of information initially sought . this study categorizes the types of information in documents being used in writing a research proposal for a master 's thesis by eleven students throughout the various stages of the proposal-writing process . the role of the specificity of the searched information in influencing its contribution is analyzed . the results demonstrate that different types of information are sought at different stages of the writing process and thus the contribution of the information also differs at the different stages . the categories of the contributing information can be understood of topicality .
