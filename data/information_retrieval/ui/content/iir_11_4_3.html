Okapi BM25: a non-binary model The BIM was originally designed for short catalog records and abstracts of fairly consistent length, and it works reasonably in these contexts, but for modern full-text search collections, it seems clear that a model should pay attention to term frequency and document length, as in Chapter 6 . The BM25 weighting scheme , often called Okapi weighting , after the system in which it was first implemented, was developed as a way of building a probabilistic model sensitive to these quantities while not introducing too many additional parameters into the model (Spärck Jones et al., 2000). We will not develop the full theory behind the model here, but just present a series of forms that build up to the standard form now used for document scoring.
