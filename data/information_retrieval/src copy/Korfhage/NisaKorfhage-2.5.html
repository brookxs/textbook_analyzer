2.5 The Fine Structure of Data

Atomic data items are items that are not to be decomposed into parts. Standards for the encoding of data provide the basis for easy integration and handling of data for multiple sources. Two major encoding systems are:

  1. EBCDIC - Extended Binary Coded Decimal Information Code
  2. ASCII - American Standard Code for Information Interchange

They both were based on the use of a byte to encode an atomic datum. A byte consists of eight bits of data. Each bits has two states, 1 or 0. Over the years, ASCII gradually became the standard for text encoding.
