10.3 Hypertext Links

Hypertext links provide a means of directly connecting two distinct pieces of text. A hypertext link consists of an identifier and a pointer. In the Web, the hypertext link is active: when the user clicks on the identifier, a transfer to the linked page occurs. Hypertext links are generally pointing to potential rich source of information and hence is useful in information retrieval.

Problems of hypertext links include:

  • Organization of material is difficult because hypertext links trigger break in flow of text.
  • Can easily lead to a "lost in cyberspace".
