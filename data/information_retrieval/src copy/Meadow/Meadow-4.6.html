4.6 Indexing of Text

An index can take on many forms. Many books contain a subject index and possibly an author index. In library science, indexing is the process of pulling subject terms from documents and placing them in a form that can be used for search. Three types of subject-describing attributes used in indexing are classification codes, subject headings, and individual words that describe the subjects. These are all examples of descriptors. Recently the focus of IR has shifted away from indexing, however indexing is still important.
